Village Camp

Ushodaya Eye Care Charity Trust has been continuously conducting free eye care camps across rural villages as part of its mission to support and uplift underprivileged communities. These camps aim to provide essential vision services to individuals with limited or no access to proper eye care.

Many villagers — especially elders, women, farmers, and children — often suffer from untreated eye problems that affect their quality of life and livelihood. Through these initiatives, the Trust brings free eye check-ups, awareness programs, and treatment facilities directly to their communities, ensuring that everyone receives the care they deserve.

  • Free Eye Check-ups and Surgeries
  • Regular Rural and School Vision Camps
  • Supported by Dedicated Donors and Volunteers
  • Vision Awareness and Education Programs

Schools Visit

Ushodaya Eye Care Charity Trust has been regularly organizing school vision camps to promote better eye health among children. These initiatives are part of our ongoing efforts to reach young students in underserved communities and ensure that vision issues do not hinder their learning and development.

Many children struggle in school due to undiagnosed vision problems, often unnoticed by parents and teachers. Through these camps, our team conducts free eye screenings to identify such issues early and provide necessary corrective measures.

  • School Children Screened: 4,76,553+
  • Free Distribution of Prescription Glasses
  • Early Detection and Treatment of Vision Problems
  • Eye Health Awareness Sessions for Students

surgeries

Ushodaya Eye Care Charity Trust has been continuously conducting free eye surgeries as part of its long-term mission to restore vision and improve lives for underprivileged patients. These surgeries are performed for individuals suffering from cataracts and other serious eye conditions that hinder their ability to work and live independently.

Over the years, thousands of patients have benefited from these initiatives, regaining clear vision and a renewed sense of confidence. Along with surgeries, the Trust ensures complete care by providing free medicines, follow-up consultations, and post-operative support for lasting recovery.

  • Free Eye Surgeries Conducted: 7,821+

Free Glasses Distribution

Ushodaya Eye Care Charity Trust has been regularly organizing Free Glasses Distribution Camps to assist individuals in rural areas who are unable to afford essential eye care. These initiatives are part of our ongoing mission to bring quality vision support to underserved communities.

Through these camps, our team conducts free eye screenings and distributes prescription glasses to those in need. Many individuals who had long struggled with poor eyesight received proper vision correction for the first time. In addition, free medication and follow-up guidance are provided to ensure lasting improvement in eye health.

  • Free Glasses Provided: 18,237+
  • Free Eye Screenings in Rural Communities
  • Medication and Post-Care Support at No Cost
  • Assistance for Financially Weaker Sections
  • Vision Awareness and Eye Health Education

Ushodaya Health Care Charity Trust is committed to providing quality eye care to underprivileged communities through the support of generous donors and volunteers.
