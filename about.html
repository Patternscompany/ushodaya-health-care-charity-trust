About Us

Where Service meets sight.Care Meets Community

Ushodaya Health Care Charity Trust has been dedicated to providing free and affordable eye care services since 2013. With the support of kind-hearted donors and skilled professionals, we conduct vision camps, surgeries, and awareness programs for underprivileged communities across Telangana.

From Then To Now

Ushodaya was founded in November 2016 with a vision to eliminate preventable blindness and ensure equitable access to quality eye care, particularly in underserved rural and semi-urban communities.

Over the last 12 years, our initiatives have reached 476,553 schoolchildren, screened 254,140 community members, provided 18,237 free spectacles, and conducted 7,821 free surgeries.

Our Vision

Bringing Quality Eye Care to Everyone, Especially Rural and Semi-Urban Communities

Eliminate preventable blindness and provide quality eye care to every individual, with a focus on underserved rural and semi-urban communities

Our Mission
  • Provide free eye screenings, sight-restoring surgery, and tele-health care in underserved villages, schools, and semi-urban areas.
  • Raise awareness of eye health and ensure early detection, treatment, and follow-up for vision problems.
  • Build sustainable partnerships with donors, professionals, and institutions to scale affordable and equitable eye care services.
Challenges We Face
  • Lack of Awareness: Many people in rural areas are unaware of the importance of eye health and delay treatment until it is too late.
  • Limited Funding: As a charitable organization, we depend on donations and sponsorships. Insufficient funds often restrict the number of surgeries and camps we can conduct.
  • Manpower Shortage: There's a shortage of trained ophthalmologists, optometrists, and volunteers, especially for remote outreach programs.
  • Logistical Issues: Transporting patients, equipment, and teams to far-off villages remains a hurdle, especially in areas with poor connectivity.
  • Sustainability: Maintaining a consistent level of care, expanding outreach, and upgrading equipment and infrastructure require ongoing support and planning.
